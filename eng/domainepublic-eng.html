Public domain

Intellectual property

free image source

The content of Ortissage's know-how is free to access, shareable and downloadable.

The know-how on the Urtica Dioica fibre aims to be accessible to as many people as possible, easily understandable and can thus be disseminated freely.

The ecological transition needs collective reflection and improvements to develop and become rooted in reality.

The public domain allows this reflection and exchange of ideas to flourish in order to solve complex problems, thanks to collective intelligence, at a much greater speed than the "classic" patent system since there are virtually no financial or practical constraints, apart from the prohibition on patenting or privatising the know-how made available.

« In intellectual property law, the public domain is a status under which intellectual assets (works, inventions, etc.) are placed. » (source)

The fact of publishing via a social network, the internet networks, paper media or events is sufficient to say that the information produced is in the public domain.

Another dimension of the public domain reinforces this dynamic.

CREATIVE COMMONS

What is Creative Commons and why is it used for Ortissage processes ?

“ Creative Commons (CC) is a non-profit organisation whose purpose is to provide a legal alternative for people who want to free their works from the standard intellectual property rights of their country, which are considered too restrictive ». (source)

There are several types of free licences. These licenses are free of charge and allow a better visibility for the notion of public domain and common good in general.

The type of licence used by Ortissage is : « Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike CC-BY-SA 3.0 ».

« This licence allows others to remix, adapt and build on your work, even for commercial purposes, as long as they credit you and grant an identical licence to their new creations. This licence is often compared to the free and open source "copyleft" software licences. All new works based on yours will carry the same licence, so all derivatives will also allow commercial use. This is the license used by Wikipedia, and is recommended for documents that would benefit from incorporating content from Wikipedia and similarly licensed projects. » (source)

This means that the processes created by Ortissage may be copied, modified, and even commercialised, provided that Ortissage is cited as the source of the information and that the same « Attribution-Share Alike CC BY-SA 3.0 » licence is granted.

The aim of this intellectual and professional orientation is to encourage the emergence of independent workshops and companies, producers of nettle fibres throughout France, Europe and beyond. The field of possibilities is wide and we need multiple responses to the demand for organic textiles and ecological materials.
